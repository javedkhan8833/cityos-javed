Key Aspects of Multi-Tenancy in Fleetbase
Data Isolation: Each organization functions as a separate tenant, ensuring secure company-level data isolation.
Onboarding: Organizations can join via the Fleetbase Console, where they create an account and define their specific organizational details.
RBAC (Role-Based Access Control): Tenants can manage granular permissions for different user roles within their own organization.
Solid Integration: For organizations using the Solid extension, each tenant is prompted to link their own Solid OIDC account to their Fleetbase organization for decentralized data storage.

 Fleetbase is designed as a multi-tenant logistics operating system, meaning it allows multiple organizations (tenants) to operate independently within the same platform while keeping their data isolated [4.5, 4.8, 4.16]. 
Here are the key aspects regarding tenants in Fleetbase:
Key Tenant Features
Data Isolation: Each organization has secure, company-level data separation [4.8, 4.16].
Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Granular permissions exist for different user roles within each tenant organization [4.8, 4.16].
Independent Instances: Businesses can deploy Fleetbase on their own infrastructure (private cloud, on-premise, AWS, etc.), essentially running their own tenant instance [4.3].
Solid Integration: The platform allows linking to Solid OIDC accounts on a per-organization basis, fitting its identity-based, multi-tenant nature [4.5]. 
Who are the "Tenants" (Users)?
Fleetbase serves various entities in the logistics sector, including: 
Logistics and supply chain companies [4.1].
On-demand delivery services (food, couriers) [4.1].
E-commerce platforms and marketplaces [4.1].
Developers building custom logistics apps [4.9]. 
In essence, any company, team, or developer managing a fleet, deliveries, or supply chain operations acts as a tenant within the Fleetbase ecosystem.
